# Intensity Distribution Policy

Source: `DurabilityFirstPrinciples`, Section 4.6.

Intensity distribution characterizes work. It is not the primary steering lever; kJ/load governance remains primary.

## Terms

- Polarized:
  - very high low-intensity volume
  - little moderate work
  - small targeted high-intensity dose
- Pyramidal:
  - high low-intensity volume
  - more moderate work than polarized
  - little high intensity

Both models require high low-intensity dominance.

## Practical defaults

- Base/general build:
  - pyramidal-leaning
  - high LIT
  - dosed tempo/sweet spot as economical race-adjacent support
  - little high intensity
- Build/performance sharpening:
  - more polarized
  - LIT remains high
  - moderate density is capped
  - one or two high-quality HI sessions only when recovered
- High fatigue:
  - keep LIT
  - reduce density and complexity
  - pause high intensity
  - use deload actively

## Decision rules

1. Durability work is primary:
   - long stable Z2, back-to-back, hard-late, and event-like fatigue-resistance work must remain repeatable.
2. Choose distribution by limiting factor:
   - aerobic ceiling/VO2 limit with stable Z2 base -> more polarized
   - economy/race-pace comfort limit -> pyramidal-leaning with dosed tempo/sweet spot
   - recovery/stress/masters limit -> more easy, less middle, HI rare or very small
3. The middle is a budget:
   - tempo/sweet spot/threshold require clear purpose
   - reduce middle once it degrades HI quality or LIT consistency
4. HI only if it is truly high quality:
   - adequate freshness
   - clean repeats
   - stable power
   - not stacked on a maximized kJ week
5. Specificity rises near the A event:
   - event-like duration
   - pace stability
   - fueling stability
   - back-to-back or hard-late relevance

Hard boundary:

- Do not use intensity distribution to override phase guardrails, S5 bands, cadence, recovery protection, or active governance.
