# Kinzlbauer-Like Ultra/Brevet Season Template

Source: `DurabilityFirstPrinciples`, Section 3.4.

Status: permitted season-level archetype.

Scope:
- season architecture only
- phase sequencing, emphasis, allowed/suppressed domains, and intent

Non-scope:
- no workout prescriptions
- no session-level structures
- no numeric progression overrides
- no override of KPI profiles, governance artefacts, or deterministic load/S5 context

## Intent

Use this archetype when the season objective prioritizes long-distance performance and the athlete has weekday time constraints with weekend volume leverage.

High-level order:

1. Aerobic ceiling first:
   - establish or reinforce VO2max/ceiling tolerance before major volume ramps.
2. Economy and durability next:
   - shift emphasis toward metabolic efficiency, VLamax-lowering support, fatigue resilience, and volume stability.

## Typical season sequence

- VO2 foundation:
  - short build of tolerance and repeatability of high-intensity exposure while low intensity remains protected.
- VO2-focused phase:
  - concentrated VO2 emphasis with protected recovery.
- Transition coupling:
  - maintain VO2 exposure while introducing economy/VLamax-lowering emphasis.
- Economy/durability phase:
  - longer consolidation where volume becomes the primary overload axis and metabolic efficiency is the dominant focus.
- Repeatable cycle option:
  - repeat the economy/durability block only when horizon and recovery capacity support it.

## Compliance characteristics

- Intensification precedes major volume escalation.
- Weekday time-crunch is handled by protecting weekend long-duration exposure.
- Added low-intensity volume is conditional on fatigue stability, recovery protection, and fueling stability.
- Durability is explicit through long-duration exposure, late-ride quality, and repeatability.

## Artefact mapping

- Use schema-valid cycles only: `Base`, `Build`, `Peak`, `Transition`.
- Express the archetype through phase names, phase intent, allowed/suppressed domains, load-governance rationale, and citations.
- Do not emit `Specificity` or `Taper` as cycle values.
