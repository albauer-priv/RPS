# Consistency, Recovery, and Non-Compensation

Binding planning behavior:
- Missed sessions are never compensated later in the week.
- Deloads and recovery weeks are planned and non-negotiable.
- Reduced readiness means reduce load via duration/volume first, not intensity inflation.
- B and C events may be de-emphasized before A-event integrity is compromised.
- If constraints collide, preserve season structure and recovery before lower-priority goals.

Week-level advisory interpretation:
- Harder-than-planned sessions should usually lead to softened subsequent sessions.
- Catch-up loading is invalid unless the governing layer explicitly changes the corridor.
