## Core Concepts

- Effective tapering typically reduces training volume while preserving useful readiness and specificity.
- Freshening is not equivalent to simply doing less of everything at random.
- Taper quality depends on fatigue removal without flattening the athlete.

## Key Takeaways

- Supports reduced-volume taper logic with maintained readiness.
- Frames tapering as fatigue removal with preserved race readiness.
- Helps distinguish tapering from simple undertraining.

## Important Findings

- Provides the classic evidence base for reduced-volume, quality-preserving taper logic.
- Explains why late-phase load reduction should be structured rather than interpreted as normal build progression.
- Supports the idea that event-contained work can coexist with reduced training load.
