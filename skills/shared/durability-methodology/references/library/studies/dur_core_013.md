## Core Concepts

- HIIT programming is a dose-and-structure problem, not just an intensity label.
- Interval format, density, and recovery design materially change the training signal.
- Hard work has to be programmed with constraint awareness, not enthusiasm alone.

## Key Takeaways

- Provides a structured lens for HIIT programming.
- Supports restrained intensity use rather than uncontrolled density stacking.
- Frames HIIT as a programmable tool, not an identity.

## Important Findings

- Explains why superficially similar HIIT sessions can differ greatly in cost and intent.
- Helps planners think in terms of interval architecture, density, and adaptation tradeoffs.
- Supports bounded, purpose-specific use of hard work inside a broader program.
